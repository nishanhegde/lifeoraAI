# Exercise Knowledge Base

## Strength Training

Strength training (resistance training) involves exercises that make muscles work against resistance. It builds muscle mass, increases metabolism, strengthens bones, and improves functional movement.

Key principles:
- Progressive overload: gradually increase weight, reps, or sets over time to continue making gains
- Compound movements (squat, deadlift, bench press, pull-up, row) engage multiple muscle groups and should form the foundation of any program
- Rest 48 hours between training the same muscle group to allow recovery

Beginner recommendation: 2–3 full-body sessions per week. Intermediate: 3–4 days using push/pull/legs or upper/lower splits.

## Cardio & Aerobic Exercise

Cardiovascular exercise strengthens the heart and lungs, burns calories, reduces stress, and improves mood via endorphin release.

Types:
- Steady-state (running, cycling, swimming at a moderate pace) — great for building aerobic base and active recovery
- HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) — alternating bursts of intense effort with rest; more time-efficient, burns more calories post-workout

WHO recommends: 150–300 minutes of moderate cardio OR 75–150 minutes of vigorous cardio per week for adults.

## Muscle Groups

Major muscle groups and primary exercises:
- Chest: bench press, push-ups, chest fly
- Back: pull-ups, rows, deadlifts, lat pulldown
- Shoulders: overhead press, lateral raise, face pulls
- Biceps: barbell curl, hammer curl, chin-ups
- Triceps: tricep dips, skull crushers, close-grip bench
- Quadriceps: squats, leg press, lunges
- Hamstrings: deadlifts, leg curl, Romanian deadlift
- Glutes: hip thrusts, squats, glute bridges
- Core: planks, dead bugs, hanging leg raises

## Recovery

Recovery is when muscles actually repair and grow stronger. Inadequate recovery leads to overtraining, injury, and stalled progress.

Key recovery strategies:
- Sleep: 7–9 hours per night is essential; growth hormone is released during deep sleep
- Active recovery: light walking, yoga, or swimming on rest days promotes blood flow without stressing muscles
- Nutrition: consume protein within 2 hours post-workout to support muscle protein synthesis
- Stretching and mobility work reduces soreness and improves range of motion

## Exercise & Mental Health

Regular physical activity is one of the most effective interventions for reducing anxiety and depression. It increases serotonin, dopamine, and endorphin levels.

Even 20–30 minutes of moderate exercise 3 times per week produces measurable improvements in mood, stress tolerance, and cognitive function.

## Building Muscle (Hypertrophy)

Muscle hypertrophy requires:
1. Mechanical tension: lifting challenging weights (70–85% of 1-rep max)
2. Metabolic stress: the "burn" from higher-rep sets
3. Muscle damage: micro-tears that repair thicker

Rep ranges: 6–12 reps per set is the classic hypertrophy range. 3–5 sets per exercise. Total weekly volume of 10–20 sets per muscle group.

Caloric surplus: gaining muscle is easier in a slight caloric surplus (200–300 kcal above maintenance). Pair with adequate protein (1.6–2.2g/kg body weight).

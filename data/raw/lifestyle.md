# Lifestyle & Wellness Knowledge Base

## Sleep

Sleep is the foundation of health. During sleep the body repairs tissue, consolidates memory, regulates hormones, and clears metabolic waste from the brain.

Adults need 7–9 hours per night. Chronic sleep deprivation (<6 hours) increases risk of obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and mental health disorders.

Sleep hygiene tips:
- Keep a consistent sleep/wake schedule, even on weekends
- Keep bedroom cool (16–19°C / 60–67°F), dark, and quiet
- Avoid screens 1 hour before bed (blue light suppresses melatonin)
- Avoid caffeine after 2pm
- Limit alcohol — it may aid sleep onset but disrupts REM sleep

## Stress Management

Chronic stress elevates cortisol, which suppresses the immune system, promotes fat storage (especially abdominal), disrupts sleep, and impairs digestion.

Evidence-based stress reduction strategies:
- Mindfulness meditation: even 10 minutes/day reduces cortisol and improves focus
- Physical exercise: one of the best stress buffers available
- Social connection: strong relationships are the #1 predictor of long-term wellbeing
- Journaling: writing about stressors reduces their psychological weight
- Breathwork: slow, diaphragmatic breathing activates the parasympathetic nervous system

## Gut Health

The gut microbiome — trillions of bacteria in the digestive tract — influences digestion, immunity, mood, and even brain function via the gut-brain axis.

Supporting gut health:
- Eat a diverse range of plant foods (aim for 30 different plants per week)
- Include fermented foods: yogurt, kefir, sauerkraut, kimchi, kombucha
- Eat prebiotic fiber: garlic, onion, leeks, asparagus, oats, bananas
- Limit ultra-processed foods, artificial sweeteners, and excess antibiotics
- Stay hydrated

## Weight Management

Sustainable weight management is about lifestyle, not short-term diets.

Key principles:
- Caloric balance: weight loss requires a moderate caloric deficit (300–500 kcal/day)
- Protein priority: high protein intake preserves muscle during weight loss and increases satiety
- Whole foods: prioritize minimally processed foods which are more satiating and nutrient-dense
- Avoid liquid calories: sugary drinks, alcohol, and juices add calories without increasing fullness
- Consistency beats perfection: adherence over months matters more than any single day

## Habit Formation

Lasting lifestyle change is built on habits, not willpower.

The habit loop: cue → routine → reward
- Attach new habits to existing ones (habit stacking)
- Start with small, achievable versions of habits (2-minute rule)
- Track progress to maintain motivation
- Design your environment to make good choices easier (remove friction)

It takes approximately 66 days on average (not 21) to form a habit, with a range of 18–254 days depending on the behavior and individual.

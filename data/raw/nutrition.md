# Nutrition Knowledge Base

## Protein

Protein is a macronutrient essential for building and repairing muscle tissue, producing enzymes and hormones, and supporting immune function. Adults generally need 0.8g of protein per kilogram of body weight per day. Athletes and active individuals may need 1.2–2.0g per kilogram.

High-protein foods include chicken breast, turkey, lean beef, fish (salmon, tuna, cod), eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, lentils, chickpeas, black beans, tofu, tempeh, edamame, and quinoa.

## Iron

Iron is a mineral that helps red blood cells carry oxygen throughout the body. Deficiency causes fatigue, weakness, and anemia. There are two types: heme iron (from animal sources, absorbed more easily) and non-heme iron (from plant sources).

Heme iron sources: red meat, liver, chicken, fish, oysters.
Non-heme iron sources: lentils, spinach, tofu, fortified cereals, pumpkin seeds, dark chocolate.

Tip: eating vitamin C alongside non-heme iron significantly improves absorption. Avoid tea or coffee within an hour of iron-rich meals as tannins inhibit absorption.

## Healthy Fats

Healthy fats support brain function, hormone production, and absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K). They include monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats.

Sources of healthy fats: avocado, olive oil, nuts (almonds, walnuts, cashews), seeds (chia, flaxseed, hemp), fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines), and dark chocolate (70%+).

Omega-3 fatty acids (found in salmon, walnuts, flaxseed) reduce inflammation and support heart and brain health.

Avoid trans fats (partially hydrogenated oils) and limit saturated fats from processed meats and fried foods.

## Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the body's primary energy source. Complex carbohydrates (whole grains, legumes, vegetables) provide sustained energy and fiber. Simple carbohydrates (sugar, white bread, candy) cause rapid blood sugar spikes.

Good complex carb sources: oats, brown rice, quinoa, sweet potato, whole wheat bread, lentils, chickpeas.

Fiber (a type of carbohydrate) supports digestive health, lowers cholesterol, and helps regulate blood sugar. Women need ~25g/day, men ~38g/day.

## Hydration

Water is essential for every cell and organ in the body. It regulates body temperature, transports nutrients, and removes waste. A general guideline is 8 cups (2 liters) per day, but needs vary with body size, activity, and climate.

Signs of dehydration: dark urine, fatigue, headache, dry mouth, reduced concentration.

Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) help retain water and support muscle function. Found in bananas, coconut water, leafy greens, and sports drinks (low-sugar options).

## Vitamins

Vitamin D: supports bone health, immune function, and mood. Synthesized from sunlight. Food sources: fatty fish, egg yolks, fortified milk. Many people are deficient, especially in winter.

Vitamin B12: essential for nerve function and red blood cell production. Found only in animal products. Vegans and vegetarians should supplement.

Vitamin C: antioxidant, immune support, collagen synthesis. Sources: citrus fruits, bell peppers, broccoli, strawberries, kiwi.

Magnesium: involved in over 300 enzymatic reactions. Supports sleep, muscle relaxation, and blood sugar control. Sources: dark leafy greens, nuts, seeds, dark chocolate, whole grains.
